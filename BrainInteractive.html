Cognitive Science & Its Impact

CEREBRAL CORTEX:

Largest part of our brain and is in charge of all our higher thinking and functioning. There are four main sections of the cerebral cortex: the frontal lobe, the parietal lobe, the occipital lobe, and the temporal lobe.

The Frontal Lobe

FUNCTION: PLANNING, ORGANIZING, PROBLEM SOLVING, ATTENTION, PERSONALITY, EMOTIONS, BEHAVIOR

You're frontal lobe is involved in the primary planning, control, and execution of all voluntary movements. It is in charge of your personality, emotions, and behavior as well as your ability to plan and problem solve. This large region is divided into three parts:

  • Premotor Cortex: in charge of of sensory movememnt and control
  • Supplementary Motor Area: plans movememnts and coordination
  • Primary Motor Cortex: sends information down through your spinal cord in order to move different parts of the body.

Object and face recognition are carried through in these visual areas. It holds the Semantic region of the brain which is resposible for grammar and language development as well.

The Temporal Lobe

FUNCTION: AUDO PROCESSING AND MEMORY FORMATION

The Temporal Lobe processes new information and stores it into our memories. It takes in our emotions and we use them to comprehend language, personality, human behvaior, and emotions. Here are more functins carried out in this region:

  • Object and Face Recognition
  • Sequencing
  • Language and Speech Development

The Parietal Lobe

FUNCTION: ACTIVATES OUR SENSES AND DISCERNS THE DIFFERENT SENSORY INFORMATION

The parietal Lobe receieves information from the outside world and uses our senses to analyze these information. It also uses our skin and nerves to detect senses.Here are more functins carried out in this region:

  • Sense of Pain
  • Interprets Visual and Motor Signals

The Occipital Lobe

FUNCTION: VISUAL PROCESSING

The Occiptal Lobe carries out visual perception and imagery in the brain. It is our most consious way of processing information and interprets vision, color, light and movement.

Background

Neuroplasticity is the way the brain is constantly reorganizing, developing, and changing itself according to your actions and environmental factors. What you think, feel, and behave like plays a huge rule in impacting your neuroplasticity and how your brain grows with you.

Our experience on online networks and websites are stored in our Limbic Brain which make these final decisions.
